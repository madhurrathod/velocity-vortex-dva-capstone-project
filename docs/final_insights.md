# Final Insights

1. Overall conversion is low at `11.27%`, so broad untargeted outreach is inefficient.
2. `Cellular` contact performs much better than `telephone`, with conversion of `14.74%` versus `5.23%`.
3. Previous campaign success is the strongest segment signal. Customers with `poutcome = success` convert at `65.11%`.
4. Conversion decreases steadily as the number of current-campaign contacts rises, from `13.04%` for one contact to `3.11%` for `11+` contacts.
5. `18-24` and `65+` age groups respond far better than middle-age bands.
6. `Students` and `retired` customers are the best-performing job segments.
7. March, September, October, and December show very high subscription rates, but these months also have lower volumes than May or July, so they should be interpreted carefully.
8. A duration-based difference exists between subscribers and non-subscribers, but duration should be treated as a post-call diagnostic rather than a pre-call targeting feature.

## Statistical Evidence

- Chi-square test shows a strong association between `contact` and `subscription`.
- Chi-square test shows a strong association between `poutcome` and `subscription`.
- Chi-square test shows a strong association between `month` and `subscription`.
- A two-sample t-test shows that call duration differs significantly between successful and unsuccessful contacts.

## Interpretation

The bank should not treat all customers or all outreach methods equally. Prior campaign history, contact channel, age band, and contact frequency all matter materially for conversion outcomes.
